Top 6 economies in the world

Our contemporary societies are driven by capitalism. Every day an uncountable number of goods and services are bought. Competition between companies is huge. China, Germany, India, Japan, the United Kingdom and the United States are the big winners based on their GDP in this game. This visualisation shows this and other indicators such as import and export, unemployment and total reserves.

GDP growth between 1991 and 2020 (%)

1. The GDP of China has only shown an increase between 1991 and 2020. The highest growth rate was in 2007 with 14.2%. The smallest increase was 2.2% in 2020.

2. The crisis in 2008, hit Germany, Japan, the United Kingdom and the United States. China and India show no sigs that they were hit by the crisis.

3. GDP in five countries show a decrease in 2020 due to corona. Only China still had an increase in GDP. The United Kingdom had the biggests decrease of GDP with 9.3%.

Import and export as share of GDP in 2020

1. Germany has the highest share of import and export of the GDP. The share of the import is 37.7% and the share of the export is 43.4%. This means that the import is worth $1039 billion and the export $1196 billion.

2. Only China and Germany have higher exports than imports. Their imports, however, are still higher than in Japan and the United States.

3. In absolute numbers, China has the highest amount of US dollars in import and export. The import and export respectively are worth $3343 billion and $385 billion.

Unemployement 1991 vs. 2020

1. Percentage of unemployment is both in 1991 and 2020 lowest in Japan. In 1991 and 2020 respectively, 2.1% and 2.8% of the labor force was unemployed.

2. The United States shows in both years high unemployment, 6.8% and 8.1% . In 2020, it has the higest unemployment of all the countries.

3. The highest decrease in unemployment as % of the labor force is the United Kingdom. Between 1991 and 2020, the unemployment dropped with 4 percentage point.
